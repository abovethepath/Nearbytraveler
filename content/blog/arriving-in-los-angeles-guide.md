# Arriving in Los Angeles — How to Experience It Like a Local

Los Angeles is one of the most visited cities on earth. It's also one of the most misunderstood.

Most visitors do the Hollywood Sign, Venice Beach, and the Walk of Fame. They eat at restaurants they found on TripAdvisor, take Ubers everywhere, and leave thinking LA is just a sprawling, car-dependent city full of people trying to be famous.

The locals know a different city entirely.

Here's how to find it.

## First 24 Hours — What Most Tourists Miss

### Skip the Tourist Neighborhoods

Hollywood Boulevard is for photos, not for actually spending time. The real LA is in the neighborhoods — Silver Lake, Los Feliz, Highland Park, Culver City, Leimert Park, Venice (the actual Venice, not the boardwalk).

Each neighborhood has its own identity. Silver Lake is creative and indie. Culver City has excellent restaurants and a strong arts scene. Highland Park is where the locals who got priced out of Silver Lake went, and it's better for it.

### Eat Where Locals Eat

LA has some of the best food in the world and most tourists never find it. The best taco trucks are in East LA and South LA, not in the tourist corridors. The best ramen is in the San Gabriel Valley. The best Korean food in America is in Koreatown.

Ask a local. Seriously — this is the fastest way to eat well in LA. A local will point you somewhere you'd never find on your own.

### Get Off the Tourist Trail at Night

The best bars and music venues in LA are in neighborhoods most tourists never visit. The Echo and Echoplex in Echo Park. The Bootleg Theater. The Satellite in Silver Lake. Small venues with incredible music and almost no tourists.

## How to Actually Meet LA Locals

This is the part nobody writes about.

LA has a reputation for being unfriendly to visitors. That reputation is mostly wrong — but it's true that the city isn't designed for accidental connection the way New York or Chicago are. People drive everywhere. Neighborhoods are spread out. There's no central square where everyone gathers.

The locals who want to meet travelers exist. There are a lot of them. You just need the right tool to find them.

Nearby Traveler (nearbytraveler.org) was built in LA by someone who has hosted over 400 travelers from 50 countries. Open it when you land and you'll see locals who are available to meet right now — people who actually live here and want to show visitors their real city.

You can also see other travelers who just arrived, if you want company from someone in the same position as you.

Mark yourself available and locals can find you. Join someone who's already available. Either way, you're no longer navigating LA alone.

## The Best Things to Do in LA That Aren't on Lists

Griffith Observatory at sunset — not a secret but worth it anyway. Go on a weekday if possible.

The Grand Central Market — downtown LA's covered market. Get the egg sandwich from Eggslut or anything from Wexler's Deli. Go for breakfast.

Abbot Kinney on a weekday — Venice's best street. Crowded on weekends, much better during the week.

A Dodgers game — if you're there during baseball season, this is non-negotiable. Dodger Stadium is genuinely one of the great sports venues in America and the crowd energy is unlike anything else in the city.

The Broad or LACMA — LA has world-class museums that don't get the attention they deserve. The Broad is free on the first Tuesday and third Thursday of each month.

A drive up Mulholland Drive at night — the view of the city from up in the hills at night is one of the most stunning things in Los Angeles. Do it.

## Getting Around

Rent a car if you can — LA is still most easily navigated by car. Uber and Lyft work well within neighborhoods but cross-city travel takes forever without a car.

The Metro is better than it used to be and connects downtown to Hollywood, Koreatown, Culver City, and Santa Monica. Worth using for specific routes.

## The Honest Truth About LA

Los Angeles rewards the traveler who puts in the effort to find the real city. The surface version is fine. The version underneath — the food, the music, the neighborhoods, the locals who actually love this city — is one of the best urban experiences in the world.

The fastest way to find it is through the people who live here.

Start at nearbytraveler.org. See who's available in LA right now. Let a local show you their city.
