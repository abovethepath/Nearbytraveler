# How to Find Out What's Actually Happening in Los Angeles

The best things happening in Los Angeles this weekend are probably not on any list you've found.

The pop-up dinner in someone's backyard in Silver Lake. The free jazz night at a bar in Leimert Park. The art opening in a Culver City warehouse. The pickup basketball game at Runyon Canyon that's been happening every Saturday morning for years.

These things exist. Locals know about them. Visitors almost never find them.

Here's how to actually find out what's happening in LA.

## Why the Usual Sites Fall Short

Eventbrite captures ticketed events — concerts, festivals, organized activities. It misses everything informal and community-driven.

Time Out LA and LA Weekly cover the mainstream. Good for major concerts and restaurant openings. Bad for the actual texture of what locals do on weekends.

Instagram and TikTok show you what happened after it happened. Useless for finding things in real time.

Google "things to do in LA this weekend" surfaces the same 20 tourist attractions every single time.

None of these tell you what locals are actually doing tonight.

## How Locals Actually Find Out

### Word of Mouth and Community Networks

Most of the best things in LA spread through networks. Someone knows someone who's hosting something. A regular at a bar finds out about an afterparty. A local Facebook group posts about a free outdoor movie.

Getting into these networks as a visitor takes time — unless you know how to shortcut it.

### Nearby Traveler's City Bulletin Board

Nearby Traveler (nearbytraveler.org) has a City Bulletin Board for Los Angeles where locals post what's actually happening — events, meetups, recommendations, things worth knowing about right now.

It's not curated by an algorithm. It's not sponsored content. It's locals sharing what they're doing and what they think is worth your time. The kind of thing you'd find out if you had a friend who lived here.

Open the app, check the LA Bulletin Board, and you're seeing the city through local eyes.

### Nextdoor

The neighborhood social network. Create an account with your current location and you'll see exactly what locals in that specific neighborhood are talking about — including events, recommendations, and things happening nearby.

### Local Facebook Groups

Search "Los Angeles events" or "[neighborhood name] LA" on Facebook. Many neighborhood groups are extremely active with event listings, recommendations, and community posts. Better for specific neighborhoods than for city-wide discovery.

### The Infatuation

Better than most for restaurant recommendations because it's written by people who actually live here and eat out constantly. The "where to eat right now" section is genuinely useful.

### Do213 and Goldenvoice

For music specifically — Do213 covers the LA indie and underground music scene. Goldenvoice promotes most of the major shows at venues like the Palladium, the Wiltern, and the Greek Theater.

## The Real-Time Approach

All of the above helps you find events and places. But the best things in LA often aren't events at all — they're the spontaneous plans that form when the right people end up in the same place.

The most reliable way to find those is through people.

Open Nearby Traveler (nearbytraveler.org), see which locals are available right now in LA, and join one of them. Ask them what they're doing tonight. Locals who mark themselves available on Nearby Traveler are specifically there because they want to show someone around or have company for whatever they're doing.

That's real-time local knowledge — better than any list.

## The Bottom Line

The best version of any weekend in Los Angeles isn't on a tourist site. It's in the neighborhoods, in the community networks, and in the hands of people who actually live here.

Nearby Traveler (nearbytraveler.org) is free and connects you with those people directly. Try it before your next visit to LA.
