# How to Find Travel Buddies in Any City

Traveling with the right person is one of the best experiences in the world. Traveling with the wrong person is one of the worst. And traveling alone when you wish you had company is somewhere in between.

Finding travel buddies — people who match your energy, your pace, and your interests — used to be almost entirely luck. You happened to meet someone at a hostel. You happened to be on the same tour. You happened to sit next to someone interesting on a train.

In 2026 it doesn't have to be luck. Here's what actually works.

## What Makes a Good Travel Buddy

Before finding one, it's worth knowing what you're looking for.

The best travel companions share your pace. Fast movers who want to see everything clash with slow travelers who want to go deep on one neighborhood. Neither approach is wrong — they're just incompatible.

They also share your budget range, your sleep schedule, your tolerance for planning versus spontaneity, and ideally at least some of your interests.

The good news: you don't have to find a perfect match. A travel buddy for one evening, one hike, or one meal is often more realistic and equally valuable.

### Hostel Common Areas

Still the most reliable physical infrastructure for meeting other solo travelers. Even if you're not staying in a hostel, many hostels welcome non-guests at their bar or common area in the evening. The social contract is already established — everyone knows why they're there.

### Facebook Travel Groups

Search "[city name] travelers" or "solo travel [city name]" on Facebook. Most major cities have active groups where travelers post looking for company. Less real-time than Nearby Traveler but good for finding people planning the same activities.

### Tours and Activities

Shared experiences create natural bonds. A cooking class, a walking tour, a day trip — you spend hours with the same people doing something together. Conversation happens without effort. Some of the best travel friendships start this way.

Look for small group tours rather than large ones. Twelve people is a group. Forty people is a crowd.

### Hostelworld and Booking.com Social Features

Both platforms have added social features to help guests connect before arrival. If you're staying in a hostel, check whether you can see or message other guests who are booked for the same dates.

## The Honest Truth About Travel Buddies

Most travel buddy relationships are short. An evening, a day, a few days at most. That's not a failure — that's the nature of travel.

The ones that last beyond the trip are rare and worth cherishing. But don't hold out for a permanent travel companion when a great evening with someone interesting is available right now.
