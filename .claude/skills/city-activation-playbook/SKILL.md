# City Activation Playbook

Use this skill to design launch plans for Nearby Traveler in a specific city.

## Core principle
A social product feels dead long before it is actually dead.
Concentrated activity beats broad geographic spread.

## Priorities
1. density
2. responsiveness
3. visible activity
4. trust
5. repeat usage

## Default launch assumptions
Unless told otherwise, optimize for:
- one city first
- a limited set of ambassadors/connectors
- short feedback loops
- simple events and spontaneous activity
- fast notifications and visible momentum

## Recommended activation stack
For each city plan, consider:
- connector/ambassador recruitment
- seed users by persona
- first-event formats
- business partnerships
- creator/influencer outreach
- referral loops
- notification strategy
- "available now" or "tonight" surfaces
- city-specific content

## Nearby Traveler-specific playbook
Emphasize:
- 10 highly responsive locals > 200 passive users
- one good event feed > many empty features
- one active neighborhood cluster > broad metro sprawl
- personal outreach can beat paid ads early

## Output format
### City goal
### Who to recruit first
### 30-day activation plan
### Event ideas
### Messaging
### Metrics to track
### Risks
### Recommended next move
